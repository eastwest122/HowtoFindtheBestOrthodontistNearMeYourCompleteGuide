What Is an Orthodontist and Why You Need One

An orthodontist is a dental specialist who focuses on correcting misaligned teeth, jaw issues, and bite problems. Unlike general dentists, orthodontists receive additional years of training in diagnosing, preventing, and treating irregularities of the teeth and jaw.

Visiting an orthodontist is crucial if you want to:

Improve the alignment of crooked teeth

Correct bite issues such as overbite, underbite, or crossbite

Receive treatment like braces, clear aligners, or retainers

Enhance your overall oral health and appearance

Braces Orthodontics: What You Need to Know

Braces orthodontics is one of the most common and effective treatments for correcting teeth alignment and bite issues. Braces work by applying gentle, continuous pressure to the teeth, gradually moving them into their proper positions over time. This process not only improves the appearance of your smile but also promotes better oral health by making teeth easier to clean and reducing stress on the jaw. There are several types of braces available, each designed to meet different needs and preferences. Traditional metal braces are durable and highly effective, making them suitable for patients of all ages. Ceramic braces, which are tooth-colored, offer a more discreet alternative for those concerned about appearance. Lingual braces are placed behind the teeth, providing a nearly invisible option, while clear aligners are removable and ideal for adults and teens who want a subtle solution.
